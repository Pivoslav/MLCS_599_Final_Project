Key terms

Teleskop (Телескоп)
Moscow journal that published Chaadaev’s first “Philosophical Letter” in 1836, provoking official reaction and debate.
Third Section (Третье отделение)
Tsarist political police (from 1826); shorthand here for surveillance, files, and fear of salons and print.
Sobornost (соборность)
Spiritual togetherness, often invoked by Slavophiles for Orthodox communal unity, not a government office, but a moral ideal.
Narod (народ)
“The people,” especially the Russian peasant people as a cultural and moral category in nineteenth-century discourse.
Neva flood (1824) (наводнение на Неве, 1824)
Historical catastrophe Pushkin treats as based on truth (his prologue); in Part One it destroys Yevgeny’s world. In-game: ordinary lives vs. embankments and plans.
The Bronze Horseman (1833) («Медный всадник», 1833)
Pushkin’s narrative in prologue + two parts: Peter’s founding vow and a lyric “I love thee, Peter’s own creation,” then the flood and Yevgeny’s fixation on the equestrian statue. The mood turns from civic pride to horror and pursuit, not one flat “message.”
Zemstvo-style councils (земство)
Historically, zemstvos were elected provincial and district assemblies created under the 1864 reform; they handled local budgets, schools, roads, medicine, and welfare within limits set from St. Petersburg. Counterfactual staging: this game uses “zemstvo-style councils” as shorthand for that idea of provincial self-rule while your salon debates in the 1830s, not as a claim that zemstvos already existed or had those powers then.
Who “you” are (кто такие «вы»)
You guide a salon and its network (gentry, minor officials, clergy), not Nicholas I. Even on the statist path you lobby, advise, withdraw, or denounce; you do not tighten censorship by decree. The Tsar and the ministries remain offstage actors your choices try to influence or appease.
Order / Reform / People (Порядок / Реформа / Народ)
Game meters: administrative continuity; law, schools, and “universal” history; material protection for clerks, peasants, and flood-side lives.
Genre (Letter I) (жанр; первое философское письмо)
Chaadaev’s first letter opens as spiritual counsel to a woman in distress, then widens into philosophy of history. The game condenses that into a debate for play.
Chaadaev’s “Europe” («Европа» у Чаадаева)
In Letter I, “Europe” often means Christendom’s moral-historical unity (duty, justice, law, order absorbed from the cradle), not the same as modern secular liberalism. The Reform meter uses institutions as a shorthand; the letter asks deeper questions about moral continuity.
