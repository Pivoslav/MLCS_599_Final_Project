## A. Chaadaev, Letter I — what the text actually *is*

| Fact | Implication for glosses |
|------|-------------------------|
| **Genre:** A **letter to “Madame,”** opening as **spiritual counsel** (distress, scruples, Catholic-universalist ecclesiology in places), *then* widening into a **diagnosis of Russian society**. | A gloss should not pretend Letter I is a public salon speech. Where the CYOA is a **dramatization**, say “Chaadaev’s letter to Madame argues…” or “In Letter I, Chaadaev tells his correspondent…” |
| **Rhetorical movement:** Comfort → doctrine of unity/succession → **social picture** (nomads, no traditions) → **history without “adolescence”** → institutions, borrowing, Providence, etc. | Good glosses **name the move**: e.g. “After the opening on Madame’s doubts, Chaadaev turns to how Russians live without fixed habits or shared public memory.” |
| **Scandal context (secondary):** First letter published Russian 1836 (*Teleskop*); official reaction, “madness” decree, house arrest—standard reference works (e.g. Britannica, encyclopedia entries) agree on outline. | Use for **historical staging** or glossary, not as a substitute for **what the letter says**. |

### A.2 Study-guide / encyclopedia layer (SparkNotes-caliber *orientation only*)

- **Pushkin’s poem:** Standard summaries (e.g. Wikipedia’s article on the poem) agree on **three parts**: **Prologue** (Peter, founding vow, ode to Petersburg), **Part One** (Yevgeny, Parasha, **1824 flood**), **Part Two** (madness, confrontation with statue, **pursuit**, **pauper’s grave**). Use only to **structure** glosses, not as copy-paste.
- **Chaadaev:** No widely used SparkNotes page was identified; **Britannica / Encyclopedia.com–style entries** stress isolation from “universal history,” East/West, publication scandal, and Westernizer–Slavophile **aftermath**. Chaadaev is **not** identical to either camp—glosses should avoid flattening him into “team Westernizer.”

## C. CYOA crosswalk — what each gloss should *do*

| Game axis | Letter I lever | Poem lever |
|-----------|----------------|------------|
| **Order** | Stability, autocracy, police/clerical discipline, fear of disorder; statist readings of “cloak” and borrowing | Peter’s vow, granite, statue, imperial city; “Destiny’s great potentate” |
| **Reform** | “Repeat the education of mankind,” law/duty/order from cradle, institutions vs. imitation | Less central unless gloss ties flood recovery / embankments / administration |
| **People** | Slave, nomads, no home, Madame’s social air | Yevgeny, Parasha, flood victims, pauper’s grave |
| **Paths** | West / Slav / statist / med use **different answers** to the same Chaadaev lines | Same poem lines support different **framings** (e.g. Order = Peter’s triumph; People = Yevgeny’s end) |

**Rule:** A gloss that only says “shame vs. flood” without **naming a beat** (e.g. “after the flood Yevgeny loses Parasha’s hut”) or **a letter move** (e.g. “Chaadaev says Russians lack the habits that elsewhere come from shared history”) fails the backlog standard.

## D. Rubric for the Step 2 rewrite

Each **Read alongside** gloss (and epilogue opening paragraphs) should ideally:

1. **Anchor:** Name **who** (Chaadaev to Madame; narrator’s ode; Yevgeny) and **where** in the work (Letter I social diagnosis; *Bronze Horseman* Part One flood, etc.).
2. **Substance:** State **one concrete claim** the primary text makes at that moment—not a vague “parallel.”
3. **Branch:** One short clause tying to **this choice** or **this meter** (which button, which path).
4. **Citation:** Quotations **verbatim Arndt** or McNally & Tempest as already in game; new quotes require spot-check in source files.
