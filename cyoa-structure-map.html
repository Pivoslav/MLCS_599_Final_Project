1. What each turn changes

Every choice advances one beat of the fiction. Three meters (Order: stability and the vertical chain; Reform: law, schools, “universal history”; People: clerks, flood-side lives, material stakes) run from 0 to 100 and start at 50. They move when you pick options, when a winter event applies its automatic shock, and once more when you choose how to frame the outcome (stability vs. reform vs. the people’s ledger). That framing adds +5 to the meter you emphasize.

Shipped game: after that +5, resolveEndingText() in js/game-app.js selects one of twelve epilogues by path (pathId) × framing (finalLeanApplied), each with its own title, body, speculative question, and discussion bullets (§7 table). §7 also keeps a legacy five-bucket reference for comparison.

2. Scene flow: what happens, in order

The chart uses in-game scene titles (two lines where helpful). Read top to bottom: four different “schools” of argument lead to four different winter 1837 chapters. Each of those rolls the die and lands on one concrete rumor (flood, censor, salon, provinces, or budget clash). Then the table sorts the winter, you choose how to tell the story, and the epilogue keys off which path you argued plus which framing you chose (twelve distinct closings).

2C. Critical audit: gaps, risks, mechanics & tool opportunities

Author-facing critique while rebuilding narrative and UI. Not all items require code, some are weekly discussion prompts.

Narrative & pedagogy

  • Epilogue voice: EPILOGUE_TWELVE bodies stay in-scene; appended winter block = misfit (if any) + richer winterEcho + path coda. Keep §7 / closing diagram aligned if titles shift.
  • Read alongside density: Repeated Letter I sentences encourage skimming; one verified quote + scene-specific gloss per block (foundation rubric).

4. The winter die: what rumor arrives

Total = six-sided die + bonus, where the bonus is the Order meter divided by 25 and rounded down (0–4). Higher Order slightly tilts toward “better” rumors for your track, unless a misfit intervenes (§5).

5. When fate “misfits” (wrong rumor)

Sometimes the story refuses to match your rehearsal: snake eyes on a strong total (die shows 1 but total ≥ 7) or a six on a weak total (die shows 6 but total ≤ 5) swaps in another track’s kind of winter. Use this for teaching contingency vs. moral parable.

7. Epilogues: twelve endings

Status: Shipped in js/game-app.js as EPILOGUE_TWELVE plus resolveEndingText() / formatEpilogueDiscussion(). On-screen epilogue titles use the same strings as the Title column below (including the Ending: prefix). The legacy five-bucket table below is kept for author reference only.

Why twelve (path × framing)

In an older stat-only design, a run could end in one of five epilogues chosen from final stat shapes alone, so a Slavophile run and a Westernizing run might receive the same title and body if their meters looked alike. That would hide the different intellectual risks each path was meant to carry.

The game keeps the same UI: the three closing choices on Sorting the outcome. It makes the epilogue path-aware: pathId (Westernizing / Slavophile / statist / mediator) × framing (Order / Reform / People) = 12 distinct outcomes. Each outcome has its own title, one-line thesis (how you retell winter), and a speculative question for section or writing that cannot be answered the same way on another path.

Stats stay important: they can still drive discussion bullets, session banner titles, achievements, and one or two sentences inside the epilogue body (e.g. flood vs. censor winter). They no longer need to be the sole selector of which “family” you get.

The twelve endings (shipped titles match UI)

PathFramingTitle (EPILOGUE_TWELVE)Thesis (how you tell the story)Speculative question
Westernizing Order Ending: The Ranks of Civilization Winter proves administration and calm must precede Europe-facing reform. Salons wait on the file. If stability is the prerequisite Chaadaev doubted, are you proving him wrong, or are you showing that his “cloak” fits the police better than the school?
Westernizing Reform Ending: The Syllabus and the Censor The moral center is law, print, and schools locked in struggle with the Third Section. Can “universal history” be studied in rooms that the censor still owns?
Westernizing People Ending: Evgeny’s Europeans Europe is not a statute but a life: clerks and flood beds must appear on the same ledger as statutes. Who is “Europe” for if the syllabus never reaches Kolomna?
Slavophile Order Ending: The Vertical Iconostasis Narod and altar align with the chain of command. Unity is read as spiritual deference. When the state claims to speak for the soil, who is left to correct it from below?
Slavophile Reform Ending: Parish Ink, Capital Light Selective borrowing (schools, oversight) without surrendering soul to abstract “rights.” At what moment does “selective” borrowing become a new orthodoxy Chaadaev would still call hollow?
Slavophile People Ending: The Soil and the Roof Love of narod is tested by material life: clerks and peasants, not only the village idea. Does Slavophile language obligate policy for the capital’s clerk, or only for the imagined village?
Statist Order Ending: The File Outlives the Salon Continuity is the achievement: ranks, seizures, the case file as the real historian. Is obedience to process a kind of civilization, or its costume while thought stays rented?
Statist Reform Ending: Commissioned Progress Europe arrives as memoranda: reform only where commissions and patrons allow. If reform lives in triplicate copies, does mankind’s “education” happen, or does paperwork replace it?
Statist People Ending: Relief Without Receipt Mercy flows from the throne and petitions. People appear as objects of grace, not of rights. When you beg the center for flood victims, who is saved: them, or the story that the throne is merciful?
Mediator Order Ending: The Minister’s Bridge The span stands only if the ministry owns the pier. Local trust is a concession, not a co-sovereign. If one pier is stone and one is rope, is “compromise” just delayed collapse?
Mediator Reform Ending: Borrowed Law, Local Accent Institutions sound foreign on paper but are spoken in provincial idiom. How long must a law be “borrowed” before it becomes native, and who declares the moment?
Mediator People Ending: The Ledger They Share Budgets and clerks can read the same line. Synthesis is material, not only rhetorical. Two ledgers, one countryside: must one always become the footnote, or is overlap itself the synthesis that Chaadaev could not name?

Implementation notes (live in bundle)

  • resolveEndingText() branches on state.pathId (default west), then state.finalLeanApplied (order / reform / people) from the chosen final_route_*.
  • Each epilogue packs title, body, specQ, and bullets; formatEpilogueDiscussion() renders discussion for the debrief UI.
  • Rare “Evgeny’s chorus” still forces people framing + extras without changing the count of primary endings.
  • When retitling an ending, edit EPILOGUE_TWELVE and this §7 table together; run verify-scenes if scene flow changes.

8. Side branches (same shape, extra flavor)

  • Petition detour: From Paper, schools, and law you can waste the autumn on forms, then return to journals. The game remembers a “scar” for callbacks.
  • Vera walks out: If you choose the harshest informer path on Files, uniforms…, Vera stops attending. Epilogue extras mention the colder room.
  • Opened with Pushkin: Tags like “literature first” tweak later lines (e.g. Vera’s callback at schools).
  • Rare fourth closing: If the group carries both Chaadaev and Pushkin tokens and People is strong enough, Sorting the outcome can offer a special line that frames letter + poem + clerk as one ledger.

9. Satchel tokens (what they represent)

  • Chaadaev’s first letter: You took the letter seriously as a program or foil.
  • Pushkin’s Bronze Horseman: You put the poem in play as a moral measure.
  • Draft charter: You favored gradual schools / moderate land reform over shock.
  • Slavophile notes / Aksakov: You committed the soil–narod answer to paper or public voice.
