Crisis card + winter roll transparency

Why it is fun: players get a “puzzle” moment, can we avoid the worst outcome?, before the fiction moves. Why it is educational: it makes thresholds and path-dependent risk explicit. Students see probability and institutions as the same conversation: “we needed Reform higher” is also “the Westernizing wager needed more print culture.”

Adjust meters

Toy crisis: “Unless Reform ≥ 60, lose 5 People when the rumor hits.”

Rumor card (winter 1837)

Ministry files cross the Neva story with your salon. Unless Reform is at least 60, clerks panic and you lose 5 People.

Show die table (transparency before the roll)

In the full game, path picks a column; the die picks a row. Showing the table teaches that randomness is bounded, not arbitrary.
